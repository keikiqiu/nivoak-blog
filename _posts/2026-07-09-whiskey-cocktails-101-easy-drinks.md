<p>You don't need to be a bartender to make great whiskey cocktails. These three classics are easy to learn and impressive to serve.</p><h2>1. Old Fashioned</h2><p>The king of whiskey cocktails. Muddle a sugar cube with 2-3 dashes of Angostura bitters. Add 2oz bourbon or rye, one large ice cube, and stir. Garnish with an orange peel. Simple, strong, perfect.</p><h2>2. Whiskey Highball</h2><p>The most refreshing whiskey drink. Fill a tall glass with ice. Add 1.5oz whiskey. Top with cold soda water. Stir gently. Garnish with a lemon twist. Use <a href="https://nivoak.com/shop/">whiskey stones</a> instead of ice for no-dilution version.</p><h2>3. Mint Julep</h2><p>The classic Kentucky derby drink. Muddle 6-8 mint leaves with 1 tsp sugar. Add 2oz bourbon. Fill glass with crushed ice. Stir until the glass frosts. Garnish with a mint sprig.</p><h2>Essential Tools</h2><p>A jigger for measuring, a muddler for herbs and sugar, and a bar spoon for stirring. Start with these and you can make most whiskey cocktails.</p>
