<p>Yes, you should put whiskey stones in the freezer — that's how they work. Freeze them for at least 2 hours before use, and keep them in the freezer between uses so they're always ready. Let's answer this and other common questions about whiskey stone storage and use.</p>

<h2>How Long to Freeze Whiskey Stones</h2>
<p>For best results, freeze your whiskey stones for at least 2 hours. Overnight is perfect. Unlike ice, whiskey stones don't become damaged by extended freezing — you can keep them in the freezer indefinitely. <h2>Can You Freeze Whiskey Stones Too Long?</h2>
<p>No. Unlike water (which expands when frozen), stainless steel and soapstone are solids — they don't change structure at freezer temperatures. You can leave them in the freezer for months with no issues.</p>

<h2>Should Whiskey Stones Be Stored in the Freezer?</h2>
<p>Yes. Keeping them in the freezer means they're always ready when you want a drink. If you store them at room temperature, you'd need to wait 2+ hours before using them.</p>

<h2>Can You Use Whiskey Stones Without Freezing?</h2>
<p>Technically yes, but they won't do anything. At room temperature, they won't chill your drink.</p>

<h2>Other Common Questions</h2>

<h3>Can whiskey stones break in the freezer?</h3>
<p>Stainless steel stones won't break. Soapstone can crack if it contains moisture that freezes and expands, but quality soapstone stones are dried properly before packaging.</p>

<h3>Can you refreeze whiskey stones immediately after use?</h3>
<p>Yes. After cleaning and drying, put them back in the freezer. They'll be ready to use again in about 2 hours.</p>

<h3>Do whiskey stones absorb freezer smells?</h3>
<p>Stainless steel stones don't absorb odors, which is another advantage over soapstone.</p>

<h3>Can you use whiskey stones in hot drinks?</h3>
<p>No — putting frozen stones into hot liquid can cause thermal shock and potentially crack some materials. They're designed for cold drinks only.</p>
