<p>Whiskey stones are typically made from either stainless steel, soapstone, or granite. The most common and best-performing material is 304 food-grade stainless steel — the same material used in professional kitchen equipment. It's safe, durable, and flavor-neutral. Here's what you need to know about each material.</p>

<h2>304 Stainless Steel</h2>
<p>This is the gold standard for whiskey stones. 304 stainless steel (also called 18/8 steel) is the same material used in high-end cookware and surgical instruments. It contains 18% chromium and 8% nickel, which makes it rust-proof, non-reactive, and completely safe for food contact.</p>

<p>Stones made from this material, like the <a href="https://nivoak.com/product/whisky-stones-gift-set-stainless-steel-ice-cubes/">Nivoak Whisky Stones Gift Set</a>, offer the best performance: they chill quickly, stay cold longer, and won't affect the taste of your whiskey in any way.</p>

<h2>Soapstone</h2>
<p>Soapstone is a natural metamorphic rock that's been used for cooking surfaces and cooling for centuries. It's softer than stainless steel and has a smooth, soapy feel. While it's safe for food use, soapstone is more porous and can absorb flavors over time. It also doesn't chill as effectively as stainless steel.</p>

<h2>Granite and Other Natural Stones</h2>
<p>Some whiskey stones are made from granite or other natural stones. These are less common because they're heavy, hard to clean, and can sometimes contain minerals that affect the taste of your drink. Always check that natural stone products are labeled food-safe.</p>

<h2>Safety Considerations</h2>
<p>Safety is the most important factor when choosing whiskey stones. Here's what to look for:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Food-grade certification:</strong> Make sure the stones are certified for food contact</li>
<li><strong>No lead or heavy metals:</strong> Cheap stones may contain harmful materials</li>
<li><strong>Smooth surface:</strong> Rough stones can harbor bacteria</li>
<li><strong>Non-reactive:</strong> Stainless steel won't react with acidic drinks like whiskey</li>
</ul>

<h2>How Material Affects Performance</h2>
<p>Stainless steel conducts heat approximately 15 times better than soapstone. This means stainless steel stones chill your drink faster and maintain temperature longer. Soapstone and granite are more about tradition and aesthetics than performance.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Are whiskey stones safe to use?</h3>
<p>Yes, when made from food-grade materials. Always buy from reputable brands that certify their products as food-safe.</p>

<h3>Can whiskey stones contain lead?</h3>
<p>Low-quality natural stone stones might contain trace minerals. Stick with certified food-grade stainless steel to be completely safe.</p>

<h3>How can I tell if my whiskey stones are food-grade?</h3>
<p>Check the product description for terms like "304 stainless steel," "food-grade," or "FDA approved." Reputable brands clearly label their materials.</p>
