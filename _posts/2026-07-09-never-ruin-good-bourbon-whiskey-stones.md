<p>You bought a nice bottle of bourbon. Maybe it was $40, maybe $80. You poured a glass, added ice, and settled in for a relaxing evening. But twenty minutes later, you were drinking something that barely resembled what you poured. Sound familiar?</p>

<p>Good bourbon deserves better than dilution. Here's how to enjoy every drop the way it was meant to taste.</p>

<h2>Why Bourbon Lovers Are Switching</h2>
<p>Bourbon is unique among whiskeys — its flavor profile is rich, sweet, and complex, with notes of vanilla, caramel, and oak that come from aging in new charred barrels. These flavors are concentrated and deliberate. Adding water changes the entire experience.</p>

<p>Many bourbon enthusiasts choose to use <a href="https://nivoak.com/product/whisky-stones-gift-set-stainless-steel-ice-cubes/">whiskey stones</a> instead of ice. The reason is simple: you get the pleasant cooling effect without altering the bourbon's carefully crafted flavor profile.</p>

<h2>Which Bourbons Benefit Most from Stones</h2>
<p>While any whiskey works with stones, certain bourbons particularly shine when chilled without dilution:</p>
<ul>
<li>High-rye bourbons (the spice notes come through more clearly)</li>
<li>Single barrel and small batch selections</li>
<li>Higher-proof bourbons (benefit from slight chilling to tame the heat)</li>
<li>Aged bourbons (the oak and vanilla notes are more pronounced without water)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Will whiskey stones scratch my glass?</h3>
<p>Quality stones have smooth edges and won't scratch your glassware.</p>
<h3>Can I use stones with cask-strength bourbon?</h3>
<p>Absolutely — stones are actually ideal for high-proof bourbons. The slight chill tames the alcohol heat without diluting the complex flavors.</p>
<h3>How do I clean stones after use?</h3>
<p>Rinse with warm water, dry thoroughly, and return to the freezer. For deep cleaning, boil stainless steel stones for 2-3 minutes.</p>
