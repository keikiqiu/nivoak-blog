<p>The glass you drink from changes how your whiskey tastes. The shape directs the spirit to different parts of your tongue and controls how much aroma reaches your nose.</p><h2>The Tumbler (Rocks Glass)</h2><p>Short, wide, and sturdy. The wide opening lets you add ice or stones easily. Best for casual drinking and whiskey on the rocks. Not ideal for nosing high-end whiskey.</p><h2>The Glencairn Glass</h2><p>The whiskey industry standard for tasting. Wide bowl narrows at the top, concentrating aromas toward your nose. Best for tasting sessions and comparing whiskeys.</p><h2>The Snifter</h2><p>Very wide bowl with narrow rim. Helps the whiskey breathe while concentrating aromas. Best for aged whiskeys and cask-strength pours.</p><h2>Which to Buy?</h2><p>Start with tumblers — most versatile. Add a Glencairn for tasting. Pair with <a href="https://nivoak.com/shop/">whiskey stones</a> for no-dilution chilling.</p>
