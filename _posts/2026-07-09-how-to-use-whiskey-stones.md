<p>Using whiskey stones is incredibly simple: freeze them for 2-4 hours, drop them in your whiskey, wait 2 minutes, and enjoy. That's it. But if you want to get the best performance and longest life from your stones, there are a few things worth knowing. Here's a complete beginner's guide.</p>

<h2>Step 1: Choose Your Whiskey Stones</h2>
<p>Not all whiskey stones are created equal. Stainless steel stones are the most effective — they chill faster and stay cold longer than soapstone or granite alternatives. Products like <a href="https://nivoak.com/product/304-stainless-steel-metal-ice-cube-box-suit-whiskey-whisky-stone/">Nivoak's stainless steel whiskey stones</a> are made from food-grade 304 stainless steel, which is rust-proof, easy to clean, and safe for long-term use.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Rinse Before First Use</h2>
<p>New whiskey stones should be rinsed with warm water and mild soap before first use. This removes any manufacturing residue. Dry them thoroughly with a soft cloth.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Freeze Properly</h2>
<p>Place your stones in the freezer for at least 2 hours. Overnight is even better. Make sure they're spread out in a single layer so they freeze evenly, not piled up in a bag.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Pour and Add Stones</h2>
<p>Pour your whiskey into a glass — a rocks glass or tumbler works best. Add 2-4 stones depending on the size of your drink. For a standard 2oz pour, 3 stones is usually perfect.</p>

<h2>Step 5: Wait Before Sipping</h2>
<p>Give the stones 2-3 minutes to transfer their cold to the whiskey. This waiting period is important — stones chill through contact, not through melting like ice. The result is a perfectly chilled drink without a single drop of dilution.</p>

<h2>Step 6: Clean After Use</h2>
<p>After use, rinse your stones with warm water and dry them. Don't use harsh detergents or abrasive sponges — they can scratch the surface. Most quality stainless steel stones are dishwasher safe.</p>

<h2>Tips for Best Results</h2>
<ul>
<li>Use 3-4 stones for a standard pour (2 oz)</li>
<li>Pre-chill your glass for even better results</li>
<li>Don't use stones directly from the freezer if your glass is thin — let them sit for 30 seconds to avoid thermal shock</li>
<li>Store stones in the freezer between uses so they're always ready</li>
</ul>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>How many whiskey stones should I use?</h3>
<p>For most drinks, 3-4 stones is ideal. For larger glasses or when you want more chill, you can use up to 6 stones.</p>

<h3>Do I need to chill the glass too?</h3>
<p>Not necessary, but pre-chilling your glass helps maintain the temperature longer. Some whiskey enthusiasts use a chilled rocks glass for the best experience.</p>

<h3>Can I use whiskey stones with cocktails?</h3>
<p>Yes! They're great in any drink where you want cold without dilution — think bourbon-based cocktails, old fashioneds, or even iced coffee.</p>
