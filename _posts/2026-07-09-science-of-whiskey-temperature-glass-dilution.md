<p>Whiskey is chemistry in a glass. Here's what's happening.</p><h2>Temperature Mutes Flavor</h2><p>Cold suppresses taste buds. That's why iced cheap whiskey tastes smooth — you're not tasting much. Ideal temperature: 60-65°F. <a href="https://nivoak.com/product/whisky-stones-gift-set-stainless-steel-ice-cubes/">Whiskey stones</a> give a slight chill without muting flavor.</p><h2>Glass Shape Controls Aroma</h2><p>80% of taste is smell. A wide bowl lets whiskey breathe. A tapered rim concentrates aromas to your nose.</p><h2>Dilution Changes Balance</h2><p>A few drops of water can release aromas. Too much water (melting ice) dilutes flavor. Stones give controlled chilling without dilution.</p><h2>High-Proof Whiskey</h2><p>Higher alcohol creates a burning sensation. Slight chill or a drop of water reduces the burn while preserving complexity.</p>
