<p>Cleaning whiskey stones is simple: rinse them with warm water and mild soap after each use, dry them thoroughly, and you're done. But there's a right way and a wrong way to maintain them. Here's everything you need to keep your whiskey stones in perfect condition.</p>

<h2>Basic Cleaning After Each Use</h2>
<p>After you've finished your drink, remove the stones from your glass and rinse them under warm running water. Use a small amount of mild dish soap and rub them with your fingers or a soft cloth. Make sure all residue is removed — whiskey can leave a slight film that might affect the taste of your next drink if not cleaned properly.</p>

<h2>Deep Cleaning</h2>
<p>Once a month, give your whiskey stones a deeper clean. For <a href="https://nivoak.com/shop/">stainless steel whiskey stones</a>, you can boil them in water for 2-3 minutes to sanitize them. Let them cool completely before returning to the freezer. Alternatively, most stainless steel stones are dishwasher safe.</p>

<h2>What NOT to Do</h2>
<ul>
<li>Don't use abrasive cleaners or steel wool — they can scratch the surface</li>
<li>Don't use bleach or harsh chemicals — they can damage the material and leave residue</li>
<li>Don't put cold stones directly into hot water — thermal shock could damage them</li>
<li>Don't leave them sitting in a wet glass overnight — this can cause stains or odors</li>
</ul>

<h2>Drying and Storage</h2>
<p>After cleaning, dry your stones thoroughly with a soft cloth. Any moisture left on the surface can freeze and form ice crystals, which defeats the purpose of using stones over ice. Once dry, store them in the freezer in their storage box or bag so they're always ready to use.</p>

<h2>How Often to Clean</h2>
<p>Clean your whiskey stones after every use. It takes 30 seconds and keeps them in good condition. If you use them daily, a quick rinse and dry is sufficient, with a deep clean once a month.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Can I put whiskey stones in the dishwasher?</h3>
<p>Stainless steel stones are usually dishwasher safe, but soapstone stones are not. Check your manufacturer's instructions.</p>

<h3>My whiskey stones smell — what should I do?</h3>
<p>Soak them in a mixture of equal parts water and white vinegar for 30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly and dry. This removes any odors without damaging the stones.</p>

<h3>Do whiskey stones need special care?</h3>
<p>Not really — just clean and dry them after each use. With proper care, quality stainless steel stones will last for years.</p>
